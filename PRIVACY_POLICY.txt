2. Data we collect
The Service does not require registration, does not collect names or email addresses, and does not use an account system. The Service does not use cookies to track users. We only collect data that is necessary for the Service to function. 

2.1. Request data
The Service collects and processes the content of requests sent to the following program interfaces (endpoints):
• /api/research/generate
• /api/research/search
The data collected includes:
• Request text — user input content
• Request parameters — technical parameters transmitted when accessing the endpoint
• Processing results — responses generated by the Service
• Timestamps — date and time of request execution

Request data is stored in an anonymized form and may be broken down into fragments for processing.

2.2. IP addresses and technical metadata
The Service may collect:
• IP address — used for security (protection against cyber attacks, DDoS) and load control (rate limiting)
• Technical metadata — information about the type of request, headers, and connection protocol
• Security logs — records of suspicious or abnormal access to the Service

3. Purposes and legal basis for processing
We process data for the following purposes:
• Provision of services — processing requests and generating responses
    • Quality improvement — increasing the accuracy and relevance of responses, evaluating model performance
• Security — protection against unauthorized access, cyberattacks, and abuse
• Load control — setting limits on the use of the Service
3.1. Legal basis for processing (Article 6 of the GDPR)
Data processing is carried out on the following grounds:
• Legitimate interest of the operator (Article 6(1)(f) of the GDPR) — ensuring the security of the Service, maintaining and improving the quality of the Service, increasing accuracy, and evaluating model performance.

We conduct a Legitimate Interest Assessment to ensure that our interests do not outweigh the rights and freedoms of users. Data processing is limited to the minimum necessary, and data is stored in an anonymized form.

4. Transfer of data to third parties
To fulfill requests, the Service uses external API providers that act as data processors in accordance with Article 28 of the GDPR:
• tavily.com — search queries and data aggregation

When transferring data to third parties:

In such cases, we ensure an adequate level of data protection by means of:

8. Data Retention
We store data for no longer than is necessary for the stated processing purposes:
• Request logs — stored in anonymized form for up to 6 months from the date of request, after which they are automatically deleted or aggregated
• Security logs — stored for up to 6 months and used to identify and prevent threats
• Anonymized aggregated data sets — may be stored indefinitely, as they do not allow the user to be identified

After the storage period expires, the data is deleted or anonymized in such a way that it is impossible to restore the connection to a specific user.

9. Data storage and deletion
9.1. Storage procedure
Request data is stored in an anonymized form. During processing, data may be broken down into fragments, reprocessed, and included in aggregated datasets to improve the quality of the Service. In this regard, it may not be possible to technically identify and extract data belonging to a specific user from the general array.

9.2. Data deletion
In certain cases, it may be technically impossible to completely delete information from aggregated or anonymized data sets. In such situations, we take reasonable measures to stop further processing of the relevant data.
Exceptions where deletion will be performed upon request:
• Copyrighted content — at the request of the copyright holder, provided sufficient evidence of copyright ownership is provided.
• Personal data — if personal data is found in the processed information array, such data will be deleted in accordance with Article 17 of the GDPR.
To submit a deletion request, please contact us using the contact details provided in Section 13.
